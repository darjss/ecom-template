# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mobile-first Mongolian shoppers browse merchant-specific catalogs and need to find, understand, and confidently purchase products with minimal friction. Merchants and their staff operate every store through a shared product interface that can remain visually consistent across stores.

## Product Purpose

Generate exceptionally fast storefronts that feel authentically designed for each merchant rather than adapted from a generic ecommerce template. The storefront earns attention through merchant-specific art direction, then converts through familiar, clear, and dependable commerce behavior.

## Operating Scale

Serve small independent merchants first. A typical Store processes roughly 10–20 Orders per day; the system should remain comfortable around 50 Orders per day and merchant audiences up to roughly 50,000 followers. These are design assumptions, not hard limits. Favor operational simplicity and basic safety over enterprise-scale abstractions or compliance theater.

## Brand Personality

Merchant-authentic, commercially confident, locally native. Each storefront expresses the merchant's own identity while the system remains recognizably trustworthy and easy to buy from.

## Anti-references

- Generic Shopify-style theme sameness where identity is reduced to logo, color, and font swaps.
- Shadcn-style card grids dressed up as commerce.
- Art-directed storefronts that hide products, prices, search, or purchasing behind novelty.

## Design Principles

- Adapt composition, not just decoration: merchant identity may reshape layout, typography, imagery, density, navigation presentation, and merchandising rhythm.
- Keep conversion behavior invariant: pricing clarity, search semantics, variant selection, cart feedback, and checkout entry remain familiar and dependable.
- Earn attention without taxing intent: distinctive art direction should make products more desirable, never harder to discover or purchase.
- Make Mongolian commerce feel native: language, currency, trust cues, delivery expectations, and mobile use are first-class rather than localized afterthoughts.
- Let invisible details compound: responsive feedback, purposeful motion, strong defaults, accessibility, and perceived speed should make every storefront feel considered.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Support full keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced motion, non-color-only states, 200% zoom, and realistic Mongolian text lengths. Merchant branding may never weaken these guarantees.
